Alpha-Gal Syndrome

Food to be avoided:

       Meat such as beef, pork, lamb, and venison       

Description:

This allergy is triggered by the consumption of mammalian meat due to a reaction to a carbohydrate called alpha-gal. Some individuals with this syndrome may also react to products containing gelatin, which is derived from animal collagen.

Food Protein-Induced Enterocolitis Syndrome

Food to be avoided

cow's milk, soy, rice, oats, and certain grains.

Description:

FPIES is a type of food allergy that primarily affects infants and young children. It typically involves delayed gastrointestinal symptoms, such as severe vomiting, diarrhea, and dehydration, triggered by specific food proteins.

Oral Allergy Syndrome (OAS)

Food to be avoided

apples, bananas, and celery.

Description:

OAS is a type of food allergy that occurs in individuals with pollen allergies, particularly to birch, ragweed, or grass pollen. It causes itching or swelling of the mouth, lips, tongue, and throat after consuming certain raw fruits, vegetables, or nuts that contain proteins similar to those found in pollen.

Red Meat Allergy

Food to be avoided

beef, lamb, and pork.

Description:

Similar to alpha-gal syndrome, some individuals develop an allergy specifically to red meats due to a reaction to alpha-gal. This allergy can cause delayed allergic reactions, including hives, itching, and gastrointestinal symptoms, several hours after consuming red meat.

Peanut Allergy

Food to be avoided

Peanuts, peanut butter, peanut oil, peanut flour.

Description:

Allergic reaction to peanuts, a legume belonging to the same family as soybeans and peas. It can trigger mild to severe allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis.

Tree Nut Allergy

Food to be avoided

Almonds, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, Brazil nuts,hazelnuts.

Description:

Allergic reaction to tree nuts, which include almonds, walnuts, cashews, pistachios, and Brazil nuts. It can cause allergic reactions ranging from mild to severe, including anaphylaxis.

Shellfish Allergy

Food to be avoided

Shrimp, crab, lobster, clams, mussels, oysters, scallops, squid.

Description:

Allergic reaction to shellfish, including crustaceans (shrimp, crab, lobster) and mollusks (clams, mussels, oysters). It can cause symptoms ranging from mild itching to life-threatening anaphylaxis.

Food to be avoided

Description:


Allergic reaction to proteins found in cow's milk, including casein and whey. It is one of the most common food allergies in children but can also affect adults. Symptoms can range from mild to severe.
